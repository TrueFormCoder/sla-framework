SLA → film / cinema (domain 7)

Cinema encodes the grammar in time, image, sound, and editing rhythm simultaneously — four simultaneous signal streams, each of which can carry different primitives. The most powerful cinematic T3 specimens are those where all four streams confirm the same collapsed state. The most powerful Type C films are those where the soundtrack and color grading claim F1 while the narrative confirms T3.

Canonical film specimens — 6 scans

Chinatown
Polanski · 1974
T3→T3 · T3 Rule honored
The story world is in T3 from the first frame (corruption, power, institutional evil). Jake tries to find order (F1 → R1 attempt). Every attempt at repair reveals deeper T3. The ending is the most precisely governed in American cinema: "Forget it, Jake. It's Chinatown." T3 Rule honored. The story world remains in T3. The film does not claim resolution. The corrupt system continues.
Arc: D1→S1→T3→T3 · R1-A (structural T3 unavailable) · notAllowedToProve: the film is not nihilistic — it is structurally honest
Manchester by the Sea
Lonergan · 2016
T3 + R1-D · canonical
Lee Chandler is in genuine T3 (the fire, the guilt, the structural impossibility of self-forgiveness). The film does not resolve this — it cannot and does not claim to. R1-D: Lee takes in Patrick (direction) but cannot return to Manchester (arrival not claimed). The final shot: Lee on the water, not back in Manchester but not still. The most precisely R1-D ending in recent American cinema.
Arc: I1→F1→T3→T3→R1-D · T3 Rule honored · canonical R1-D specimen · the film earns its ending because it doesn't overclaim it
It's a Wonderful Life
Capra · 1946 · contested
R1 or Type C — requires Mahnke analysis
The most debated resolution in the corpus. George Bailey is in T3. Clarence shows him the counterfactual (what Bedford Falls would be without him). The structural question: does the ending resolve George's T3 (genuine R1) or does it show him the Type C alternative while leaving the structural T3 (financial pressure, Mr. Potter's power, the town's dependence on one person) intact? The money arrives. But Mr. Potter keeps the money from the beginning. The structural dysfunction of the town's economy is unchanged.
Contested: R1 for George personally (REMEMBER restored, I1 reactivated) · T3-A for Bedford Falls systemically · the film may be both simultaneously · requires domain specification before scan is conclusive
Marriage Story
Baumbach · 2019
T3→T3 · both characters honest
The marriage is in T3 from before the film begins (the list-reading exercise establishes this immediately — Love and its failure coexisting). The divorce proceeds. Neither character experiences false repair. The final scene (Nicole reading Charlie's unsent letter) is T3→T3 honest: they still love each other; the marriage is still over; neither fact resolves the other. Henry ties his father's shoe. R1-D implied for both — but not claimed.
Arc: T3 (pre-existing) → T3 (acknowledged) · T3 Rule honored · both characters in R1-D by the final frame · the film's grammar matches its subject precisely
La La Land
Chazelle · 2016 · contested
Type C critique or Type C enactment?
The "what if" finale is the most structurally interesting sequence in recent film. The film builds to T3 (Sebastian and Mia's relationship seals). The "what if" shows them together, happy, at the piano. The question: is the film endorsing the Type C fantasy (showing it as the better outcome) or critiquing it (showing it as the road not taken, then returning to T3 reality)? The final shared glance before they separate again answers this: the film returns to T3, acknowledging the Type C alternative was a fantasy. T3 Rule honored — barely.
Contested · most likely: T3 + Type C sequence as internal character fantasy, returned to T3 reality · the ending's grammar depends on whether the audience believes the final glance or the final montage
Cast Away
Zemeckis · 2000
R1-D · canonical directional
Chuck's T3 is the island (isolation, survival, near-death). He escapes (T3→R1 initiated). He returns to find Kelly has moved on (the ◇ sealed in his absence — a temporal near-miss at civilizational scale). The final shot is the most structurally honest directional image in mainstream cinema: Chuck at a crossroads, literally, looking in all directions. He doesn't know which way to go. He might go any of them. R1-D: direction set (he's back in the world, moving); arrival not claimed. Wilson as I1 lost — never recovered.
Arc: I1→F1→T3→R1-D · T3 Rule honored · the film earns its ending by refusing to name the destination · canonical R1-D specimen

THREAT V11
Film adds a new complexity: four simultaneous signal streams, any of which can carry a different primitive from the others
Text, music, and design each have one primary signal stream (prose, sonic, visual). Film has four simultaneously: image, editing rhythm, diegetic sound, and performance. The most structurally complex Type C films are those where the score claims F1 while the image confirms T3 (It's a Wonderful Life is contested precisely because of this). The unified scanner currently asks domain-level questions; it doesn't ask which stream is making which claim. Strategy: add a stream-conflict question (Q2 in the film protocol) that specifically asks whether all four streams are in agreement. Stream conflict = S1 at the minimum; if the conflict is unacknowledged, it may be T3.
