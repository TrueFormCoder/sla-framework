## Key Convergences

### Stage 11 → D3 Interpretive Fog → Register Chimera risk
When truth feels like betrayal, information is being filtered through an Interpretive Fog (D3) that reframes accurate signals as hostile. This is Register Chimera in governance terms: the system is producing Loop-mode output dressed as Sharpen-mode output.

### Stage 19 → CORDIA pathway blocked
When correction is destroyed (Stage 19), the CORDIA review layer becomes unavailable. This is why CORDIA must be protected by governance rule — once captured, it cannot be used to restore itself.

### Stage 41 → ANUBIS cannot catch it
Stage 41 ("make coherence possible but never normal") is invisible to point-in-time gate checks. ANUBIS fires on individual claims. Stage 41 is not a failed claim — it's a field where correct claims require perpetual effort while false claims flow effortlessly. The ELEOS-CT Habituation Audit is the only tool that catches Stage 41. This is the governance gap identified in W-BIND-003.

### Stages 9, 27, 33 all map to SAC
The Mature Evil Chain returns to the burden-transfer pattern at three different scales: individual (Stage 9, generational transmission), systemic (Stage 27, selection capture), and institutional (Stage 33, accountability capture). SAC at 93.5 is the ecosystem's highest-scoring construct because it names the pattern that appears at every layer.
