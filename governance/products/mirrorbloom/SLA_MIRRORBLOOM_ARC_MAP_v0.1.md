## ARC PATTERN SOMATIC SIGNATURES

### ARC-001: Ordeal Arc
**Governing question:** Can the body survive descent and re-emerge?

| Arc Phase | SLA Primitive | Somatic Signature | ELE Markers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | F1 | Steady breath, regular pulse, spine upright | B-open, P-steady, S-active |
| Separation | T1 | Sharp pulse spike, breath catch, gut lurch | P-spike, B-arrest, G-lurch |
| Descent | T3 (transient) | Shallow breath, elevated then flat pulse, spine absent | B-shallow, P-depleted, S-absent |
| Return initiation | R1 | First deep breath after arrest — the body knows before the mind | B-deepen, P-restart |
| Reconstitution | I1 | Breath full, pulse regular, spine returns, gut settles | B-full, P-settle, G-anchor, S-restore |
| Elevated flow | F1 (elevated) | All four markers active at higher baseline than origin | B+P+G+S all elevated |

**MirrorBloom Micro protocol:** The R1 initiation breath (B-deepen) is the signal
that the body is preparing to exit T3. It arrives before cognitive recognition.
MirrorBloom Micro Protocol → BREATHE-FIRST is the somatic name for this moment.

**Clinical edge:** In Ordeal Arc, the body's B-arrest in T3 must not be interrupted
by premature S-restore attempts. Forcing spine structure before breath returns = R1-C
dressed as recovery.

### ARC-002: Recognition Collapse (Type B Closure)
**Governing question:** What does the body do when R1 is structurally unavailable?

| Arc Phase | SLA Primitive | Somatic Signature | ELE Markers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loop running | L2 | Pulse repeating at elevated baseline, breath shallow but steady | P-loop, B-cycling |
| Avoidance | F4 | Breath quickens, pulse irregular, gut active (warning signal unheeded) | B-quick, P-irregular, G-signal |
| Convergence | P2 | Gut distortion — something is wrong but cannot be named | G-warp, B-hold |
| Recognition fracture | T1 | Gut lurch (violent truth signal), pulse spike, breath arrest | G-lurch, P-spike, B-arrest |
| Collapse | T3 (structural) | All four markers arrested — the body freezes | B-arrest, P-flat, G-overwhelm, S-absent |
| P4 active | P4 | One marker preserves: S-thin — a spine-thread that observes without acting | S-thin (witness posture) |
| No R1 | — | No B-deepen arrives. The breath does not return. | — |

**MirrorBloom Micro protocol:** The S-thin (witness posture) in Type B Closure is not
coherence — it is the somatic form of P4. The body has not repaired. It is witnessing.
Do not interpret S-thin as recovery.

**Somatic governance note:** The body in Recognition Collapse is not failing.
It is accurately encoding a situation in which R1 was unavailable.
The freeze is structural honesty, not dysfunction.
T3 WEAP-001 applies: do not demand R1 demonstration from a body in B-arrest.

**MirrorBloom protocol that applies:** STEADYING — not repair, not return, but
holding the person in witnessed freeze without demanding movement.

### ARC-003: Basin Clearance
**Governing question:** What does the body do when total structural loss is required before emergence?

| Arc Phase | SLA Primitive | Somatic Signature | ELE Markers |
|---|---|---|---|
| Original order | S3 | All four markers coordinated, spine leading | S-lead, B-P-G following |
| Erosion | T2 | Progressive S-weakening, P-irregular, B-shallowing over time | S-degrade, P-irregular, B-shallow |
| Total collapse | T3 (structural_home) | All markers at minimum — body has relinquished structure | B-P-G-S all minimum |
| Residual emergence | S4 | Single G-signal (gut fires first — not yet coherent, but not silent) | G-first-signal |
| New structure | new S3 | B-returns as G-confirms, then P-steadies, then S-rebuilds | G→B→P→S sequence |

**Critical observation:** The sequence in Basin Clearance REVERSES the normal sequence.
Normal SLA restoration: RECEIVE → GUIDE → COMPILE → REMEMBER → INFER → EMIT.
Somatic parallel: G (gut/truth) fires first, before B (breath/allowance).
This is why forcing B (breath work) before G (gut signal) in a Basin Clearance
state is structurally incorrect — it imposes the wrong arc's protocol.

**MirrorBloom Micro protocol:** GROUND-FIRST — in Basin Clearance, gut signal precedes
breath recovery. Do not lead with breathing protocols. Wait for the gut signal.

## Diagnostic Protocol: Arc Identification from Somatic Reading

A practitioner reading B/P/G/S can identify which arc is active:

```
IF B-arrested AND P-flat AND G-overwhelmed AND S-absent:
  → T3 active. Identify mode:
    S-thin present? → ARC-002 (Recognition Collapse, P4 active)
    G-first-signal? → ARC-003 (Basin Clearance, emergence beginning)
    B-maintained?   → ARC-004 (Polycentric Field, sustained fragmentation)
    ELSE            → ARC-001 (Ordeal, awaiting B-deepen)

IF G-surge AND S-fracture:
  → ARC-008 (Shadow Integration, confrontation phase)

IF P-loop AND B-cycling (no arrest):
  → ARC-002 building (L2 accumulation phase, pre-collapse)
  OR ARC-005 (Covenant Loop, L2-healthy mode)
  Disambiguation: G-signal quality
    G-warning (unheeded) → ARC-002 building
    G-steady             → ARC-005 functioning

IF G-first-signal after all-minimum:
  → ARC-003 (Basin Clearance, residual emergence)
  Do NOT introduce B protocols. Wait for G→B sequence.
```
