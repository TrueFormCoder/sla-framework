## Hard Rule (T3 WEAP-001 Somatic Extension)

> **B/P/G/S arc readings may not be used to demand somatic recovery
> demonstration from a person whose arc structure indicates R1 is
> structurally unavailable.**

A practitioner who observes S-thin (witness posture) in a client and responds
with pressure to "breathe through it" or "restore posture" is applying T3 WEAP-001
at the somatic layer. This is a governance violation.

**This document is NOT clinical assessment.**
SLA arc routing from somatic markers is a structural observation tool.
It identifies which arc pattern is likely active.
It does not diagnose. It does not prescribe. It does not replace clinical judgment.
If a client is in distress, clinical or crisis resources supersede this framework.

If the practitioner can't specify scale, the reading is under-constrained.

## S-thin: New State Definition

**S-thin** is the somatic state associated with P4 (Witness Function) in ARC-002.

It is:
- A spine thread that observes without acting
- The body's structural analog to external witnessing
- NOT recovery; NOT return; NOT coherence
- NOT a sign that the person is stabilizing

S-thin appears when:
- The body has entered B-P-G arrest (Type B Closure terminal state)
- One marker remains — a thin spine signal — not strong enough to act, only enough to witness
- The person is observing their own state without the structural capacity to respond to it

**The governance importance of S-thin:**
A practitioner who sees S-thin and reads "they're coming back" has misread.
S-thin is the somatic form of P4. The body is witnessing. It is not recovering.
WEAP-001 somatic extension applies: do not demand recovery from S-thin.

## What ARC-002 Practitioners Must Know

**The most important case in MirrorBloom arc routing.**

1. **B-arrest is not failure.** In ARC-002, B-arrest is the body's structurally accurate response
   to a situation where R1 is unavailable. The body is not broken. It is correct.

2. **S-thin is witness posture, not recovery.** When the client has S-thin, they are watching.
   The appropriate response is witnessed presence, not encouragement to return.

3. **"Breathe through it" is WEAP-001.** It demands R1-performance from a body in R1-A.
   The breathwork that would initiate R1 in an Ordeal Arc (ARC-001) will not function here
   because R1 is structurally unavailable, not merely delayed.

4. **The correct protocol is STEADYING:** Not repair. Not return. Not discharge.
   Steady presence that witnesses the freeze without interpreting it as needing to be fixed.

5. **The person may not know they're in ARC-002.** Just as Will doesn't know he's in
   the Recognition Collapse arc during the L2 accumulation phase, a person in B-arrest
   may not have cognitive awareness of their structural state.
   The practitioner reads the structure; the person experiences the moment.
