## The Problem This Solves

MemorySeal™ currently stores and authenticates memory artifacts without a
structural classification system. Two artifacts — a wedding vow recording and
a trauma witness record — can both be MemorySeal entries, but they require
different consent protocols, different display contexts, different access gates,
and different handling when the subject is a child or the subject has died.

SLA arc patterns give LMI what it's been missing: a structural taxonomy for
classifying *what kind of memory event a given artifact represents*.

The arc type is not a label the archivist applies — it is a structural observation
from scanning the artifact. The arc type governs the artifact's handling protocol.

## Memory Artifact Types by Arc Pattern

### Type 1: Transformation Record (Arc-001: Ordeal Arc)
**What it is:** A record from an entity that descended into T3 and returned.
The record captures the arc — not just the outcome.

**Structural signature:** The artifact contains before/after evidence of
structural change. The entity at the end is structurably different from
the entity at the start. R1 is present as a structural event, not just claimed.

**Examples:**
- Recovery narrative from addiction or illness
- Life-review record at end of life covering a major transformation
- A person's account of leaving a controlling system

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Subject and designated Level 1 continuity holders
- Display: Full arc narrative is appropriate; before-state material is contextualized
- Child Consent Ladder: Child subjects require parental consent for before-state material;
  the arc's I1 reconstitution is the primary protected identity
- Synthetic Memorial Doctrine: May be used for living memorial with consent;
  after-state I1 is the canonical identity, not before-state T3

### Type 2: Witness Record (Arc-002: Recognition Collapse / Type B Closure)
**What it is:** A record from an entity inside T3 with P4 active. R1 did not arrive.
The artifact witnesses a state that could not be changed.

**Structural signature:** The artifact contains T3 material with P4 active
(external observation present). There is no R1 in the artifact.
The artifact itself may be the only P4 witness that exists.

**Examples:**
- The P2L voicemail (sonic witness record — ELEOS state at moment of prevented contact)
- A terminal diagnosis recording made when the patient knew R1 was unavailable
- A witness statement from a person who observed an event they could not stop

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Highly restricted — subject + Level 0 continuity holder minimum
- Display: Requires explicit consent from subject; P4 material cannot be displayed
  without the subject's understanding that the record is being witnessed
- Child Consent Ladder: Maximum protocol — T3 material involving minors requires
  both parental consent AND child assent when child is capable of assent
- Synthetic Memorial Doctrine: TYPE-2 RECORDS MAY NOT be used to generate
  synthetic voices or interactive memorials without explicit pre-mortem consent
  documented with separate witness. The freeze state cannot be re-animated.
- Archive Grade classification (formerly Black Label): Default Archive Grade
  until consent chain is verified

**CMN (Collapse Music Notation) note:**
When the artifact is sonic (audio recording, voicemail, music), the sonic content
itself carries structural information. A voice recording made during T3
encodes the ELEOS state of the speaker at that moment.
This is inadvertent structural evidence — the speaker did not intend to create
a governance artifact, but the recording constitutes one.
The three-stage voicemail degradation protocol (F1 → L2 → T3 → P4 residual)
is a CMN specimen of the Witness Record type.

### Type 3: Threshold Record (Arc-003: Basin Clearance)
**What it is:** A record from an entity that experienced total structural loss
and persisted. The artifact captures what survived the basin.

**Structural signature:** The artifact contains material from before the clearance,
through the basin, and after S4 emergence. The entity at the end is structurably
different from the original S3 — it is not a restored version of the origin.

**Examples:**
- A family record spanning cultural displacement or generational trauma
- A personal record from someone who survived total loss (home, identity, community)
- A record of an institution that collapsed and reconstituted differently

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Subject + community record holders (collective scale arc)
- Display: The new S3 is the canonical identity. The origin S3 is historical context.
  Neither is pathological.
- Child Consent Ladder: Special consideration for child subjects of collective-scale
  clearance events — the child may not have language for the arc they survived
- Synthetic Memorial Doctrine: The reconstituted identity (new S3) is the memorial
  identity. Do not generate memorials from the basin state.

### Type 4: Positional Record (Arc-004: Polycentric Field)
**What it is:** A record from one perspective within a multi-agent field
where truth is positional. The artifact is explicitly one view among many.

**Structural signature:** The artifact cannot be understood without knowing
the position from which it was recorded. Multiple records from the same
event will be structurally inconsistent — this is not error, it is the arc.

**Examples:**
- Testimony from one party in a contested family situation
- An oral history from one generation's view of a collective event
- A record made by someone whose relationship to the truth was partial

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Standard, but the display MUST include positional framing
- Display: "This record reflects [subject]'s position within a complex event.
  Other participants held different structural positions."
- Positional framing is not a disclaimer — it is structural accuracy.
- Synthetic Memorial Doctrine: Polycentric records may be included in memorials
  only with explicit positional framing. Presenting one positional record as
  the account = D3 (Frame Bleed) at memorial scale.

### Type 5: Correction Record (Arc-005: Covenant Loop)
**What it is:** A record of deviation and return within an ongoing commitment.
The covenant persists; the artifact records a correction cycle.

**Structural signature:** The artifact has a before-state (deviation from I1/C4),
a correction event (R1 → I1 renewal), and an after-state (same covenant, refreshed).
The covenant itself is structural background.

**Examples:**
- An amends record within a long-term relationship or community
- A record of an institution's formal acknowledgment of deviation and return
- A recovery checkpoint in an ongoing treatment relationship

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Parties to the covenant
- Display: Both the deviation and the return are equally important.
  A record that shows only the correction without the deviation = R1-C risk.

---

### Type 6: Altitude Record (Arc-006: Spiral Refinement)
**What it is:** A record of the same entity encountering the same structural
challenge at a different developmental altitude. The record is meaningful only
in context of the series.

**Structural signature:** The artifact is one pass of a recurring L3 spiral.
The pattern is recognizable but the entity's response has shifted.

**Examples:**
- Annual self-assessment records that reveal gradual I1 elevation
- A series of records around the same relational pattern at different life stages
- A record series showing progressive mastery of a skill or practice

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Subject across time; series must be preserved together
- Display: Single records from altitude series are low-information without context.
  The series is the specimen; individual records are data points.
- Synthetic Memorial Doctrine: Altitude series are ideal for living memorials
  that show trajectory rather than moment.

---

### Type 7: Emergence Record (Arc-007: Generative Rupture)
**What it is:** A record of what came from a disruption. The artifact captures
the new structure that the disruption made possible.

**Structural signature:** Before-state = C3 (established norm). The disruption is
present as S1/S2. The artifact focuses on S4 — what emerged.

**Examples:**
- A record of creative breakthrough from crisis
- A record of an institutional innovation that came from structural conflict
- A record of the new thing that was impossible before the break

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Standard
- Display: The disruption context is important but not the focus.
  Emergence Records are forward-looking; the new S3 is the canonical state.

---

### Type 8: Integration Record (Arc-008: Shadow Integration)
**What it is:** A record from an entity that owned previously unacknowledged content.
The artifact captures the boundary expansion — the enlarged I1.

**Structural signature:** The artifact may contain I2 material (the shadow content
before integration). The I1 at the artifact's end is demonstrably larger than at start.

**Examples:**
- A personal record from someone who came to understand a pattern they had suppressed
- A record of the moment a long-denied truth was acknowledged
- A written account of shadow integration from therapeutic or contemplative process

**LMI handling protocol:**
- Access: Subject only, unless subject explicitly grants access
- Display: The I2 material (shadow content) requires the most careful contextualization.
  It is not evidence of pathology — it is structural evidence of what was held.
- Child Consent Ladder: I2 material involving children has the highest protection level.
  A child's unacknowledged content must not be made visible to others without
  the child's own consent — not just parental consent.
- Synthetic Memorial Doctrine: Integration Records may be used for living memorials.
  The enlarged I1 is the canonical identity; the I2 material is personal context,
  not part of the memorial.

## CMN Note: Voicemail as Type 2 Witness Record

The P2L voicemail requires specific arc-type handling under this taxonomy:

The voicemail is Type 2 (Witness Record) — T3 + P4 material at sonic scale.
The voicemail was not composed as a memorial artifact. It is inadvertent evidence.
Its content encodes Will's ELEOS functional state at the moment of prevented contact.

Under LMI Synthetic Memorial Doctrine:
- The voicemail may NOT be used to generate a synthetic voice of Will
- The voicemail IS a governance artifact: sonic evidence of the Witness Record
- Its display in P2L requires Type 2 consent framing: "This is a record of what
  could not be changed, preserved as structural evidence, not as a memorial."

This is why the P2L screenplay issue identified in active session notes states
"11:04 AM voicemail needs reframing as inadvertent sonic evidence" — the current
framing treats it as emotional content (R1-frame) when it is structural evidence
(P4-frame). The reframe is not stylistic; it is arc-type accurate.
