## Canonical Definition

**SAC — Sacrificial Transfer**

A state in which burden is displaced from its generator to a secondary party who absorbs it without having created it. The bearer carries load that is not rightfully theirs. The absorber may appear to be the problem. The generator may be invisible, absent, or structurally protected from the cost they created.

**Canon condition (mandatory for any SAC classification):**
Burden Ledger must be completed before SAC is assigned. Required fields:
- Current bearer (who is carrying it now)
- Generator (who created the load)
- Beneficiary (who benefits from the displacement)
- Rightful portion (what portion, if any, the bearer legitimately carries)
- False transfer (yes/no — is the displacement without consent or acknowledgment?)
- Dispute flag (yes/no — is the generator assignment contested?)

**If dispute flag = yes:** route to CORDIA before classification. Do not penalize disputed burden.

**ANUBIS link:** J<4 + NW>3 triggers mandatory Burden Ledger precheck. ANUBIS cannot fire on J/NW criteria when SAC-pattern Burden Ledger is unresolved.

**REGFW link:** If distress signal is present alongside SAC state, REGFW-006 takes precedence. Help is routed before SAC classification.

## NEAREST CONFUSIONS — MANDATORY COMPANION

No practitioner may use SAC without reviewing this table first.

### SAC vs TRG (Tragic Convergence)

| | SAC | TRG |
|---|---|---|
| **What's happening** | Burden was created by someone else and displaced | Multiple causes converged to close the path back |
| **Primary pain** | Carrying what isn't yours | The path you needed isn't available |
| **The wrong move** | Penalizing the bearer for the load | Declaring collapse final before testing repair |
| **The right move** | Return load to generator; redistribute rightfully | Test every viable repair route first |
| **Key question** | "Who made this?" | "Is return actually unavailable, or just hard?" |

**Confusion risk:** A person in TRG (genuinely trapped by converged factors) may also be in SAC (carrying displaced burden from the same system). Co-occurrence is possible. Do not collapse one into the other. TRG addresses the closed path; SAC addresses the misplaced weight.

---

### SAC vs MAR (Martyr-Witness)

| | SAC | MAR |
|---|---|---|
| **What's happening** | Someone is carrying another's load | Someone is bearing witness to harm at personal cost |
| **Primary position** | Bearer / absorber | Witness / testifier |
| **The wrong move** | Scoring the absorber as the problem | Requiring suffering as the credential for testimony |
| **The right move** | Identify generator; redistribute | Route distress before scoring testimony |
| **Key question** | "Is this their burden?" | "Is their suffering being used as evidence?" |

**Confusion risk:** A witness in MAR may also be absorbing displaced burden (SAC). The cost of witnessing can itself become a burden displacement. When both are present: address REGFW-006 (distress bypass) first, then SAC Burden Ledger.

---

### SAC vs COV (Covenant Coherence)

| | SAC | COV |
|---|---|---|
| **What's happening** | Burden displaced from generator to bearer | Agreement present but terms may be dead or coerced |
| **Primary structure** | Load transfer without consent | Relational contract, alive or broken |
| **The wrong move** | Treating the bearer's overload as personal failure | Enforcing terms one party no longer recognizes |
| **The right move** | Burden Ledger; rightful redistribution | Living-terms check; context drift check |
| **Key question** | "Who created this weight?" | "Do both parties still recognize what was agreed?" |

**Confusion risk:** A burden can be displaced through a covenant. "You agreed to carry this" can be used to legitimate a false transfer. When an agreement is cited as justification for burden-bearing, run both SAC Burden Ledger AND COV living-terms check.

---

### SAC vs DHA (Dharmic Alignment / Role-Capacity Alignment)

| | SAC | DHA |
|---|---|---|
| **What's happening** | Wrong-person carries the load | Right-person in role, but load may be disproportionate |
| **Primary question** | "Whose burden is this?" | "Is this mine and is it sustainable?" |
| **The wrong move** | Calling false transfer "alignment" | Calling extraction "calling" |
| **The right move** | Identify generator; redistribute | Burden proportionality check; opt-out protection |
| **Key question** | "Did they create this?" | "Is this renewable or depleting?" |

**Confusion risk:** DHA ("you're built for this") is the most common narrative used to legitimate SAC. When DHA language appears alongside high NW and low J, run the SAC Burden Ledger. "You were made to carry this" does not establish that the load is rightfully theirs.

---

### SAC vs INI (Initiatory Spiral)

| | SAC | INI |
|---|---|---|
| **What's happening** | Displaced burden from another source | Proportionate challenge producing genuine growth |
| **The key difference** | The bearer didn't generate the load | The person chose or can meaningfully engage the challenge |
| **The wrong move** | Calling displaced burden "your growth challenge" | Calling harm "initiation" without agency |
| **Key question** | "Did this come from outside?" | "Do they have agency in this?" |

**Confusion risk:** The most dangerous SAC misuse is reframing displaced burden as a personal growth challenge. "This hardship is making you stronger" can mask SAC. Check agency (INI requires it) and check burden origin (SAC requires tracing it).

## SAC DEPLOYMENT RULES

1. Never classify SAC without completing the Burden Ledger.
2. Never penalize the bearer before running the generator check.
3. Never let DHA language justify a SAC pattern without proportionality check.
4. When distress is present: REGFW-006 first. SAC classification after care.
5. When generator is disputed: CORDIA before classification.
